Injectable treatments — Botox, Dysport, dermal fillers, and the growing family of biostimulators — generate more revenue for the average med spa than almost any other category. They are fast to perform, repeatably priced, and when done well, they create clients for life. The challenge is not the treatment itself. It is getting the right clients through the door, getting them to come back every three to four months, and building a practice where word of mouth does the heavy lifting.

Most med spas undermarket their injectable services for one of two reasons: they either rely too heavily on deep discounts that attract price-shoppers instead of loyalists, or they post inconsistently on social media and call it a marketing strategy. Neither approach builds a sustainable book of business.

This guide covers what actually works in 2026 — from positioning your injectable practice to building the digital systems that turn first-time Botox clients into annual spend of $3,000 or more.

## Know Your Injectable Client Before You Market to Them

Effective marketing starts with knowing who you are talking to. Injectable clients are not a monolith. A 28-year-old getting preventative Botox for the first time has completely different motivations, price sensitivity, and decision-making process than a 45-year-old who has been getting filler for six years and is ready to discuss volume restoration.

The most productive injectable client segments for most practices:

**The Prevention-First Client (Ages 25–34).** This group sees Botox and light filler as part of a wellness routine rather than a cosmetic fix. They respond to education-forward messaging, subtle "before and after" visuals that show natural results, and the language of prevention rather than correction. They are deal-sensitive at first but loyal if they love their results.

**The Maintenance Client (Ages 35–50).** Already converts. Already understands the value. This segment responds to convenience (easy booking, consistent provider availability), loyalty programs that reward their regularity, and upgrades — the conversation from Botox to Sculptra, from lip filler to full facial balancing. They drive the highest annual spend per patient.

**The Event-Driven Client.** Booking ahead of a wedding, reunion, milestone birthday, or major life event. High urgency, willing to spend, but often lapsed after the event unless you have retention systems in place. Converting event clients into recurring clients requires deliberate follow-up.

**The Referral Client.** Comes in because a friend or family member sent them. Highest conversion rate of any source. Arrives with trust already established. Make their first visit flawless and they become the next referral source.

Build your messaging around these segments rather than a generic "we do Botox" approach. The more specific your communication, the more it resonates — and the higher your return on every marketing dollar.

## Build a Google Presence That Earns Trust Before They Call

The majority of new injectable clients start their search on Google — either searching the procedure ("Botox near me"), the provider type ("med spa Naples FL"), or the result they want ("natural lip filler Fort Myers"). Your Google presence needs to be ready to convert at every stage.

**Google Business Profile.** Your GBP listing is the most visible real estate you have in local search. For injectable practices, this means:

- A primary category of "Medical Spa" or "Cosmetic Procedure Facility" — not just "Beauty Salon"
- A complete list of services including individual injectable treatments
- At least 50 professional photos (exterior, interior, consultation room, treatment photos — no graphic clinical images)
- An active Q&A section that answers the top 10 questions new clients ask: "Does it hurt?" "How long does it last?" "What is the difference between Botox and filler?" "Do you accept insurance?"
- Weekly posts highlighting new client offers, before and afters (with signed consents), or educational content

Practices with GBP profiles that include 100+ photos and 50+ reviews consistently rank in the Google Map Pack for local injectable searches. If you are not in the top three Map Pack results, you are invisible to a significant portion of your local market.

See [how to optimize your Google Business Profile](/blog/google-business-profile-optimization-guide) for a step-by-step walkthrough.

**Review Volume and Quality.** In the aesthetic industry, reviews are the single biggest trust signal for a first-time client. The goal is 4.8 stars or above on a minimum of 75 reviews. Below 50 reviews, prospects feel uncertain. With 100+ reviews and a consistent 4.9, your practice practically sells itself online.

Build review generation into your post-treatment workflow — not as an afterthought, but as a systematic step. Send a review request SMS within 24 hours of treatment. Include a direct link. Make it one tap. See [how to automate review generation](/blog/review-generation-automation-guide) for implementation details.

## Instagram and TikTok: The Injectable Decision Engine

Instagram and TikTok are not optional for injectable practices in 2026. Clients routinely research providers on these platforms before, during, and after their decision to book. Your social presence is your reputation outside of reviews.

### What Actually Performs for Injectable Practices

**Before and After Content.** Still the most engaged-with content category in aesthetics, when done right. The difference between good and mediocre before/afters:

- Natural, consistent lighting in both photos (same spot, same distance, same expression)
- Subtle results that look like "better you," not "obvious procedure"
- Signed patient consent and HIPAA-compliant posting (no last names, no identifiable information in the caption unless the patient explicitly consents in writing)
- Context in the caption — treatment details, number of units/syringes, follow-up timeline

One strong before/after will outperform five generic stock photos every time.

**Educational Reels and TikToks.** Questions like "What is the difference between Botox and Dysport?" and "How long does lip filler last?" generate genuine search volume inside social platforms, not just on Google. A 60-second video answering a common question builds trust faster than an advertisement. The injector who consistently shows up in these searches becomes the default choice when a follower is ready to book.

**Day-in-the-Life and Behind-the-Scenes.** Clients choose a provider as much as a practice. Content that shows the injector's approach, their training, their thought process, and their genuine care for clients is powerful differentiation. This content converts browsers into bookings because it builds a connection before there is ever an appointment.

**Real-Time Results.** Before/immediately after video clips (when appropriate and consented) perform extremely well on TikTok. The immediate "wow" moment, particularly for lip filler or jawline definition, is shareable and drives inbound inquiries.

### What Does Not Work

- Stock photos of syringes and needles
- Generic "come try our Botox" posts with no education or personality
- Inconsistent posting (one month of great content, two months of silence)
- Posting before/afters without explicit patient consent and HIPAA-compliant captions

On the HIPAA front: publishing patient photos without proper signed consent — even if the client verbally said "go ahead" — exposes your practice to significant liability. Get written photo release consent before the appointment, not after. For detailed guidance, see our [HIPAA-compliant social media marketing guide](/blog/hipaa-compliant-social-media-marketing).

## The New Client Offer: Convert Without Cheapening Your Brand

Almost every successful injectable practice runs a new client offer. The question is how to structure it so you attract committed clients rather than coupon-seekers.

**What to Avoid.** Deep discounts (30–50% off Botox) consistently attract the wrong clients — those who are primarily motivated by price, not results, and who will leave for the next Groupon the moment your introductory offer ends. These clients require the most time to convert, generate the least word of mouth, and often leave reviews complaining about price after the offer expires.

**What Works Better:**

_Consultation credit, not discounting the treatment._ Offer a $50 or $75 consultation credit that applies toward their first treatment when they book an in-person assessment. This pre-qualifies the client (they are serious enough to book an appointment), builds a real relationship before any needles are involved, and gives you the chance to recommend the right treatment rather than just fulfilling a discounted service.

_Bundle introductory packages._ Instead of discounting a single treatment, offer a starter package that introduces the client to your practice properly: a Botox treatment plus a 30-minute provider consultation plus a complimentary at-home care kit. Price the bundle slightly below what those items cost separately — but frame it around the experience, not the discount.

_Referral-forward first offer._ "Book your first appointment and bring a friend — you both receive X." This structures your first-client offer around generating another client immediately, which dramatically improves the economics of your new client acquisition.

The goal of any first-visit offer is to trigger a return visit. Every discount should be designed with that second appointment in mind.

## Email and SMS: The Retention Engine for Injectable Clients

Acquiring a new injectable client costs significantly more than retaining an existing one. The math on lifetime value is stark: a client who comes in for Botox every four months and adds one filler treatment per year generates $1,800–$3,500 in annual revenue with essentially zero acquisition cost. Building systems to retain them is your highest-ROI marketing activity.

### The Post-Treatment Follow-Up Sequence

Send this within 48 hours of every injectable appointment:

1. **Same-day thank you.** Brief, personal, no pitch. "Thank you for coming in today — it was great seeing you. Let us know if you have any questions as your treatment settles."

2. **48-hour check-in.** Ask how they are feeling, address common questions about bruising or swelling normalizing, confirm they know to contact you with any concerns. Attach a care instructions PDF.

3. **Two-week results check.** This is where touchup conversations happen naturally. "How are you loving your results? Most clients notice full effect by now — if you have any questions or want to discuss a touchup, we are here." Include a direct booking link.

4. **Six-week re-booking trigger.** "Your next Botox appointment is coming up in about 6–8 weeks to maintain your results. [Book now] while your preferred time is available." Clients who receive this message before they start noticing the treatment wearing off re-book at significantly higher rates than those who are chased after the fact.

Automate these touchpoints through your practice management software or a CRM integration. Manual follow-up does not scale. See [automating patient communication](/blog/automating-patient-communication-guide) for a detailed walkthrough on setting this up.

### Birthday and Anniversary Offers

Send a personalized offer 30 days before each client's birthday. This is not a deep discount — it is a gesture. "We want you to feel your best on your birthday — here is something from us." A $50 credit, a complimentary add-on treatment, or priority booking during the birthday week. These messages have significantly higher open and conversion rates than any other promotional email.

Similarly, track each client's first-visit anniversary and send an appreciation note with a special loyalty offer. Clients who feel valued by their providers recommend them. This is where reviews and referrals come from.

### Reactivation Campaigns for Lapsed Clients

Identify clients who have not booked in six or more months. These are not lost clients — they are lapsed ones, and a targeted campaign recovers a meaningful percentage.

The most effective reactivation message is personal and direct: "We have not seen you in a while and wanted to check in. Are you happy with your results? Is there anything we can help with? We have [appointment time] available for you this week if you are ready to come back in."

Avoid the templated "we miss you!" blast emails. They feel generic because they are. See [patient reactivation campaigns for med spas](/blog/patient-reactivation-campaigns-aesthetic-practices) for a complete step-by-step framework.

## Google Ads for Injectable Services: What to Know

Paid search is effective for injectable practices — particularly for capturing high-intent searches like "Botox near me" or "lip filler [city name]." But it requires strategy to be cost-effective.

**Campaign structure that works:**

- Separate campaigns for Botox/neurotoxins and dermal fillers
- Tight geographic targeting (10–15 mile radius around your practice, not an entire metro)
- Call extensions so mobile users can book directly from the ad
- Landing pages specific to the treatment (not your homepage) with a single, clear booking CTA
- Remarketing audiences for people who visited your site but did not convert

**What to budget.** In competitive markets (Miami, Dallas, New York), CPCs for injectable keywords can run $8–$20 per click. In mid-size markets (Naples, Fort Myers, Scottsdale suburbs), $3–$8 is more typical. A $1,500/month budget managed well can generate 5–15 new injectable clients monthly in most mid-size markets — enough to produce strong positive ROI when lifetime value is factored in.

**The mistake most practices make.** Running ads to a generic homepage or booking page with no treatment-specific messaging. The ad says "expert Botox in Naples" and the landing page says "Welcome to [Practice Name], we offer a full range of treatments." The prospect bounces. Build landing pages for your top three or four injectable services and run traffic to those pages specifically.

## Building a Referral Machine for Injectables

The best injectable practices are referral practices. When a client loves their results and their provider, they tell their friends — and those friends book with trust already established.

Referral marketing does not happen by accident. It requires:

**A referral ask that feels natural.** Train your team to ask at the follow-up appointment, not immediately after treatment: "We are so glad you are happy with your results. We would love to meet your friends — here is a referral card that gives them [X] off their first visit and gives you [X] credit toward your next one." Simple, clear, zero awkwardness.

**A referral tracking system.** If you cannot track where your referrals come from and close the reward loop (sending the credit, acknowledging the referral), the program falls apart. Your CRM should handle this automatically. See [building a referral program](/blog/referral-program-guide) for setup guidance.

**Social sharing incentives.** Clients who share their before/after results with their followers (with proper consent) send qualified traffic at zero cost. For clients who are willing to share publicly, a small incentive — upgrade, credit, or complimentary add-on — pays for itself ten times over. One post from a well-followed local influencer or enthusiastic client can generate more inquiries than weeks of paid advertising.

## Loyalty Programs: The Retention Structure That Works for Injectables

Loyalty programs work extremely well for injectable practices because the service is inherently recurring. Every Botox client is a built-in repeat customer — the only question is whether they repeat with you or with someone else.

**Membership model.** Charging a monthly membership fee ($49–$149/month) that provides members with discounts, priority booking, and quarterly treatment credits is the most effective loyalty structure for high-frequency injectable clients. When a client is paying $99/month, they are psychologically committed to using those benefits — and they are not going to your competitor. See [med spa membership programs](/blog/med-spa-membership-programs-guide) for a complete framework.

**Points-based rewards.** Simpler to implement and understand. Each dollar spent earns points that convert to treatment credits. Works well for practices not ready to launch a membership program.

**VIP tiers.** Gold/Platinum tiers based on annual spend create aspirational loyalty. Clients in the top spend tier receive exclusive benefits — early access to new treatments, a dedicated provider relationship, complimentary add-ons at appointments. This model works particularly well for high-value filler clients.

Whatever structure you choose, the goal is the same: make it more valuable to stay with your practice than to try a competitor, and make that value visible at every appointment.

## Tracking Your Injectable Marketing: The Metrics That Matter

Without measurement, you cannot optimize. Track these metrics monthly:

| Metric                                | What It Tells You                   | Target                       |
| ------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **New injectable clients per month**  | Volume of acquisition               | Track trend month over month |
| **New client source**                 | Where they found you                | 40%+ from referral/organic   |
| **First-visit conversion rate**       | % who book after consultation       | 70%+                         |
| **90-day retention rate**             | % who return within 90 days         | 60%+                         |
| **12-month retention rate**           | % who are still active after a year | 50%+                         |
| **Average annual revenue per client** | Client lifetime value driver        | $1,500–$3,000                |
| **Referral rate**                     | % of clients who refer someone      | 15%+                         |
| **Review generation rate**            | % of clients who leave a review     | 20%+                         |

The relationship between retention rate and revenue is more powerful than most practices realize. Moving from 40% 12-month retention to 60% does not add 20% more revenue — it can double revenue over three years as your retained client base compounds. Retention is your highest-leverage metric.

## The 90-Day Injectable Marketing Sprint

If you are starting from scratch or relaunching your injectable marketing, here is a focused 90-day plan:

**Month 1 — Foundation.**

- Audit your Google Business Profile and add 50+ photos if needed
- Launch a review request SMS automation post-treatment
- Create a new client offer structured around consultation value, not deep discounts
- Identify your top three injectable services and build Instagram Reels templates around them

**Month 2 — Activate.**

- Post 3x/week on Instagram minimum — one educational, one before/after (consented), one practice culture
- Launch your automated post-treatment follow-up sequence
- Ask every happy client for a referral at their follow-up visit
- Set up lapsed-client reactivation emails for anyone inactive 6+ months

**Month 3 — Optimize and scale.**

- Review your new client source data — double down on what is working
- Launch a Google Ads campaign for your highest-value injectable keyword
- Introduce a membership or loyalty program if not already in place
- Identify your top 20% of clients by revenue and give them a VIP experience upgrade

By the end of 90 days, you should have clear data on where your injectable clients come from, which channels convert, and what your retention rate looks like. Everything from there is optimization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I run before/after photos on social media without HIPAA issues?**

Yes — but only with proper written patient authorization. A signed photo release that explicitly covers social media use and specifies the platforms is required. Verbal consent is not sufficient. Many practice management systems include consent forms that cover this, but review them carefully to ensure social media platforms are named. Do not include names, birth dates, or other identifiable information in captions unless the patient has specifically consented to that disclosure.

**Should I offer Groupon or deal sites for Botox?**

Generally, no. The economics rarely work: the margin on a deeply discounted Botox unit leaves little room for anything beyond covering your cost, the clients who find you through deal sites have the lowest retention rates of any acquisition channel, and the time spent on high-volume, low-margin clients is time not spent on your ideal patients. The exception is a very specific, limited new-client offer — not a public coupon — that is structured around a consultation experience rather than a price point.

**How often should I post on Instagram for my med spa?**

Three to five times per week is the sweet spot for most injectable practices. More important than frequency is consistency — posting every day for a month then going dark for three weeks is far less effective than posting reliably three times a week indefinitely. Build a content calendar and batch-create content monthly so you are never scrambling.

**What is the best platform for injectable marketing in 2026?**

For aesthetics, Instagram and TikTok remain the most effective platforms for before/after content, education, and building a following. Google (GBP and Ads) is essential for capturing high-intent local search. Email and SMS are your highest-ROI channels for retention. Pinterest converts surprisingly well for filler content — procedure inspiration drives significant traffic to practice websites. You do not need to be everywhere — pick two social platforms and your retention channels, and do those well.

**How do I compete with the large med spa chains?**

On personalization and relationship. Large chains compete on price and brand recognition. You compete on knowing your client's name, their treatment history, their preferences, and their goals. A client who has been coming to you for three years and trusts you implicitly is not going to leave for a chain — even if the chain is slightly cheaper. Build relationships, not just results.
