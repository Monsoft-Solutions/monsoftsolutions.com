Google Local Services Ads (LSAs) sit at the very top of Google search results — above all paid search ads and organic listings. When someone in your service area types "plumber near me," "HVAC repair," or "tax accountant," the businesses in the LSA pack appear first, with a green "Google Guaranteed" badge that signals instant trust to potential customers.

What makes LSAs fundamentally different from traditional Google Ads: you pay per lead, not per click. A qualified phone call or message from a local prospect is what triggers a charge. No click-through to your website. No paying for people who were just browsing. You pay when a real local customer reaches out.

For local service businesses, LSAs typically deliver some of the lowest cost-per-acquisition numbers of any digital advertising channel — because the people who find you via LSAs are already searching with high intent.

If your business provides local services and you're not in the LSA section, you're effectively invisible for the most valuable searches in your market. This guide breaks down exactly how to get in and how to optimize for the best results.

## What Google Local Services Ads Actually Are

LSAs are a verified, pay-per-lead advertising product built specifically for local service businesses. They appear as a distinct section at the top of Google search results — sometimes called the "Local Services pack" — and show a curated set of businesses that have passed Google's verification process.

Each LSA listing displays:

- Business name, phone number, and city
- Average star rating and number of reviews
- The **Google Guaranteed** or **Google Screened** badge
- Whether the business is currently open
- Service categories offered

The badge is the distinguishing feature. It tells the searcher: Google has vetted this business. They've passed background checks, license verification, and insurance confirmation. If a customer is unhappy with the service, Google may reimburse them up to $2,000. That's a powerful trust signal that generic ads simply cannot replicate.

## How LSAs Fit Into Google's Local Search Results

Understanding where LSAs sit in the search results page helps you see why they matter so much:

**Position 1 (very top): Local Services Ads** — 2–5 verified businesses with the Google Guaranteed badge. Paid per lead.

**Position 2: Google Search Ads** — traditional pay-per-click ads from any advertiser (no verification required).

**Position 3: Google Maps Pack / Local Pack** — the three-business organic local results tied to your Google Business Profile.

**Position 4+: Organic search results** — standard SEO-ranked web pages.

Research from Google and independent studies consistently shows that LSAs capture 25–40% of all clicks for high-intent local service searches. For a plumber, electrician, or HVAC company, that's a massive share of the most valuable traffic.

The businesses that dominate local search appear in multiple sections simultaneously: LSA at the top, a Google Search Ad below it, and in the Maps pack. That level of visibility requires effort — but LSAs are often the highest-ROI entry point.

The list of eligible categories has grown significantly over the past few years. If you're not sure whether your business qualifies, check directly at [ads.google.com/local-services-ads](https://ads.google.com/local-services-ads) — it shows availability by category and zip code.

For Southwest Florida businesses (Naples, Fort Myers, Cape Coral, Bonita Springs), LSAs are available for most home service categories and are growing in professional services. HVAC, pest control, and landscaping companies in particular see strong results given the year-round demand in the SWFL climate.

## Google Guaranteed vs Google Screened: What's the Difference

There are two badge types, and which one you get depends on your business category:

**Google Guaranteed** (green badge with a checkmark)

- For home service businesses (plumbers, electricians, HVAC, etc.)
- Requires: background check on business owners, license and insurance verification
- Google's promise: if a customer is dissatisfied with covered work, Google may reimburse them up to $2,000 (lifetime)
- The reimbursement program applies to work booked through the LSA — not just any work you do

**Google Screened** (blue badge)

- For professional service providers (lawyers, financial advisors, real estate agents)
- Requires: background check and license verification
- No financial reimbursement guarantee (professional services have different liability structures)
- Still signals that Google has verified the provider's credentials

Both badges significantly increase click-through rates compared to unverified listings. The Google Guaranteed badge in particular tends to convert at higher rates because of the financial guarantee's psychological impact on potential customers.

## Setting Up Your LSA Profile: Step by Step

### Step 1: Check eligibility

Go to [ads.google.com/local-services-ads](https://ads.google.com/local-services-ads), enter your business category and service area, and confirm LSAs are available for you.

### Step 2: Create your business profile

Set up your LSA profile with:

- Business name, address, and phone number
- Service categories (be specific — "Emergency Plumbing" and "Water Heater Installation" are better than just "Plumbing")
- Service area (choose the zip codes or cities you actually serve — don't over-expand)
- Business hours
- Years in business

### Step 3: Submit verification documents

This is the bottleneck for most businesses. You'll need to provide:

- Business license (varies by state/category)
- Proof of insurance (general liability, workers' comp where required)
- Pass a background check through Google's partner (Pinkerton for most markets)

The background check runs on business owners and employees who go into customers' homes. Plan ahead: verification typically takes 1–4 weeks depending on how quickly you submit complete documentation.

### Step 4: Set your weekly budget

LSA budgets are set weekly, not daily. Google will estimate how many leads your budget is likely to generate based on your market and category. Start conservatively — you can always increase it once you understand your cost per lead.

Most home service businesses start with $150–$300/week to get enough data. Professional services may need more depending on competition.

### Step 5: Go live and monitor

Once verified, your LSA listing goes live. For the first few weeks, focus on:

- Responding to every lead immediately
- Requesting reviews from your first LSA customers
- Disputing any invalid leads promptly

## How LSA Pricing Works

The pay-per-lead model is genuinely different from how most digital advertising works — in a good way for service businesses.

**What counts as a lead:** An inbound phone call or message from a potential customer in your service area who found you through your LSA listing.

**What it costs:** Varies significantly by category and market. Approximate ranges:

| Category          | Typical Cost Per Lead |
| ----------------- | --------------------- |
| Plumbing          | $18–$45               |
| Electrical        | $20–$50               |
| HVAC              | $25–$60               |
| Pest Control      | $15–$35               |
| Law (general)     | $35–$100              |
| Real Estate       | $40–$120              |
| Financial Advisor | $40–$150              |
| Roofing           | $25–$75               |

These are market averages. Costs in competitive markets like Miami or Orlando run higher; costs in SWFL markets like Fort Myers can be lower.

**Invalid lead credits:** If you receive a lead that doesn't qualify — wrong service area, spam, wrong category, or a customer who calls by accident — you can dispute it within 30 days. Google reviews disputes and issues credits for legitimate ones. Regularly disputing invalid leads improves your net cost per lead significantly.

**No contract:** There's no minimum spend, no cancellation fee, and no long-term commitment. You control your weekly budget and can pause or stop at any time.

## What Affects Your LSA Ranking

Google doesn't publish an exact ranking algorithm for LSAs, but the factors that demonstrably matter include:

**Proximity** — How close your business is to the searcher's location. This is partly why specifying accurate service areas matters; don't claim territory you don't actually serve.

**Responsiveness** — How quickly you answer calls and respond to messages. LSAs track your response rate and speed. Businesses that answer quickly rank better. The target is sub-5-minute response time for any lead.

**Reviews** — The quantity and recency of your LSA reviews. Note: LSA reviews are separate from your Google Business Profile reviews. You need to actively build your LSA review count.

**Profile completeness** — A fully filled-out profile (all service categories, photos, business description) ranks better than an incomplete one.

**Business hours** — Being marked as "open now" gives a ranking boost. If you have after-hours answering, set your hours to reflect actual availability.

**Budget availability** — If you exhaust your weekly budget, your listing disappears for the rest of the week. Consistent budget coverage prevents gaps.

**Lead quality signals** — If customers consistently dispute leads or give poor ratings after working with you, your ranking can decline.

## Building Reviews for Your LSA Profile

Reviews are the most impactful factor you can control — and they're a separate system from your Google Business Profile reviews.

**How to request LSA reviews:**

- From the LSA dashboard, go to "Reviews" and send a direct review request link to customers after a completed job
- Or use the LSA mobile app to send requests from your phone immediately after service

**Strategy:**

- Request a review within 24–48 hours of job completion when the experience is still fresh
- Aim for 20+ reviews before significantly scaling your budget — more reviews mean better ranking and higher conversion
- Respond to all reviews (positive and negative) through the LSA dashboard
- Review velocity matters; consistent new reviews signal an active, growing business

You cannot transfer your Google Business Profile reviews to LSA — they're distinct systems. However, both contribute to your overall Google presence, and the review-requesting habits you build for LSA will improve your GBP rating simultaneously.

## Handling LSA Leads Effectively

Your lead-handling behavior directly affects your ranking and your cost-per-lead. Here's what best practice looks like:

**Answer the phone.** When an LSA lead comes in, your phone rings. Answer it — or call back within minutes if you miss it. Google's data shows that businesses with response times under 5 minutes rank significantly higher and convert a much greater percentage of leads into booked jobs.

**Mark leads accurately.** In the LSA app, mark each lead as "Booked," "Not Booked," or "Can't help." This data helps Google understand your booking rate and refine who they send leads to.

**Dispute invalid leads within 30 days.** Legitimate disputes include:

- The customer is looking for a service category you don't offer
- The call was a solicitation or spam
- The customer is in a location outside your service area
- You already served this customer (existing relationship)
- The lead had wrong information (called to reach a different business)

Invalid leads you don't dispute still cost you money. Build the habit of reviewing your lead log weekly and disputing anything that doesn't qualify.

**Follow up on messages.** If a customer sends a message instead of calling, respond quickly in the LSA interface. Message leads count the same as phone leads for ranking purposes.

## Integrating LSAs with Your Broader Marketing

LSAs work best as part of a layered local marketing strategy — not as a standalone channel.

**LSA + Google Search Ads:** Running both means you can appear twice on the results page — once in the LSA section and again in the Search Ads section below it. Double visibility, especially for high-intent searches. Budget them separately and use Search Ads to capture the searches that fall outside your LSA categories.

**LSA + Google Business Profile:** Your GBP is the foundation. Keep it updated with fresh photos, accurate hours, and regular review responses. A strong GBP supports your LSA ranking and captures customers who find you via the Maps pack or organic search.

**LSA + [Review automation](/blog/review-generation-automation-guide):** Automating your review requests ensures you're building both your LSA and GBP review counts consistently. More reviews mean better rankings and higher conversion across all Google surfaces.

**LSA + [CRM automation](/blog/crm-automation-small-business-guide):** When an LSA lead comes in, your CRM should capture it automatically, trigger a follow-up sequence if the lead isn't booked, and route it to the right team member. Many CRMs integrate directly with the LSA platform or via Zapier/Make.

## SWFL-Specific Context

For businesses in Southwest Florida, LSAs are particularly valuable for a few reasons:

**Year-round service demand:** HVAC companies, pest control services, and landscapers in Naples, Fort Myers, and Cape Coral see steady demand across all 12 months (not just summer or winter peaks). LSAs provide consistent lead flow without seasonal strategy complexity.

**Snowbird season surge:** October through April brings a major population increase to SWFL. Your LSA listing captures the wave of new arrivals searching for service providers they don't have yet. Increasing your weekly budget starting in October is a smart move to capture this demand spike.

**Relatively lower competition:** Compared to major metros, LSA competition in most SWFL categories is still moderate. Businesses that get in now can establish strong ranking positions before their markets get more competitive.

**Healthcare and wellness growth:** With the expanded healthcare category rollout in LSAs, some med spas and aesthetics practices are beginning to qualify in Florida markets. Check eligibility if you're in that space — early movers typically capture the best positions before competition intensifies.

## Common Mistakes That Cost You Money and Rankings

**Setting the budget too low.** If your weekly budget runs out by Wednesday, you're invisible Thursday through Sunday. Use Google's lead volume estimates to set a budget that keeps you visible all week.

**Ignoring the response time requirement.** If you consistently miss calls and don't call back quickly, your ranking drops. If you can't answer every call, set up a [24/7 AI phone agent](/blog/ai-phone-agents-small-business-guide) or answering service to capture leads when you're busy.

**Neglecting review requests.** Reviews don't appear automatically — you have to ask for them. Build a post-job workflow for requesting LSA reviews. Even a simple text message with a direct review link gets solid response rates.

**Not disputing invalid leads.** Many business owners assume they can't get credits and don't try. Legitimate disputes are approved at a high rate. Log and dispute every invalid lead.

**Over-expanding your service area.** Claiming all of Southwest Florida when you really only serve Naples and Marco Island hurts your ranking (proximity factor) and generates leads you can't serve profitably. Keep your service area tight and accurate.

**Skipping the mobile app.** The LSA mobile app lets you manage leads, send review requests, and track performance from anywhere. A job that's completed in the field can get a review request sent before you've even packed up your truck.

## What to Expect in the First 90 Days

**Weeks 1–2 (Verification):** Submit your documents and wait for approval. Use this time to collect business photos for your profile and prepare your review request workflow.

**Weeks 3–4 (First leads):** Your listing goes live. Expect a learning period where Google tests your listing and measures your response rate. Answer every call, dispute invalid leads, and request reviews from your first LSA customers.

**Weeks 5–8 (Optimization):** With some review data and response history, your ranking starts to stabilize. Adjust your service categories based on which lead types are converting best. Fine-tune your budget based on actual lead costs.

**Weeks 9–12 (Scaling):** If your cost-per-lead is positive, increase your weekly budget to capture more volume. Continue building reviews consistently. At this stage, you should have enough data to forecast leads and budget with reasonable accuracy.

Most home service businesses see their first profitable LSA leads within the first 2–3 weeks of going live. Professional services may take slightly longer due to higher per-lead costs and longer sales cycles.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do Google Local Services Ads replace regular Google Ads?**

No — they're complementary. LSAs are only available for certain service categories and use a per-lead model. Google Search Ads cover a broader range of search intent, use pay-per-click pricing, and are available to any business. Most local service companies run both: LSAs for the highest-intent local service searches, Search Ads for everything else.

**How much budget should I start with?**

For home services in SWFL, $150–$250/week is a reasonable starting point. This typically generates 4–10 leads per week depending on category and competition. Use Google's lead volume estimate tool during setup to calibrate your starting budget, then adjust after 3–4 weeks of data.

**What if my business category isn't eligible for LSAs?**

Focus on [optimizing your Google Business Profile](/blog/google-business-profile-optimization-guide) and running Google Search Ads instead. Also check back periodically — Google regularly expands LSA eligibility to new categories and markets. Medical spas, for example, have been rolling out in select markets.

**How long does verification take?**

Typically 1–4 weeks, depending on how quickly you submit complete documentation. The background check through Google's partner (Pinkerton) usually takes 3–7 business days once submitted. License and insurance verification can take a week or more if your state's licensing database is slow to respond.

**Can I use LSAs if I don't have a website?**

Yes. LSAs have their own profile system and don't require linking to a website. However, having a website strengthens your overall Google presence and improves conversions from customers who find you via other channels. A basic website is worth the investment.
